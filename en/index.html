How would you describe your playing?

Think of a typical moment when you pick up the handpan.

I follow instructions and try to replicate patterns. I'm often unsure what to do next.
I know some basics and recognize recurring patterns. Sometimes I land a nice groove, but I often lose it again.
I can practice independently and have developed my own routines. I consciously plan what I want to play, but still need time to think.
Music often flows intuitively through me. I respond spontaneously to what emerges and sense what the moment needs – but I'm searching for more depth.

What's holding you back the most from playing the way you want?

I lack the rhythmic fundamentals – I often lose the pulse or don't know how to build variations.
I can play patterns, but it doesn't sound like me. I want to find my own expression.
I've been playing for a while, but depth is missing. I want to understand what lies behind the music – not just technique.
I have a specific blockage or have reached a point where I can't progress on my own.

How do you prefer to learn?

At my own pace, with materials I can explore myself – without fixed schedules.
In a group with regular live sessions – the accountability and exchange help me stay on track.
Individually with personal guidance – I need someone who addresses my specific situation.
Through deep immersion – multi-day formats where I can fully dive into the music.

What's your biggest goal with the handpan?

Build a solid foundation – clean technique, steady rhythm, truly understand the basics.
Find my own musical voice – compose, improvise, express myself authentically.
Deeper presence and awareness in playing – music as a path to inner development.
Be part of a community – connect with other players and learn from each other.

Philosophy

The Art of Seeing Life as a Work of Art

Beyond Technique

"Out beyond ideas of wrongdoing and rightdoing, there is a field.
I'll meet you there.
When the soul lies down in that grass, the world is too full
to talk about."

— Rumi

The Handpan Schule des Lebens is not a music school in the traditional sense. It is a space for people who want to understand what music is beyond learning patterns, beyond right and wrong.

It is about the heart of sound. About the art of deep listening. About the touch within expression. Here, music becomes a mirror of the inner landscape – and learning becomes a journey into your true spiritual nature.

A New Way of Learning is Needed

In school, we learn to equate "learning" with imitation and conformity. Yet learning is proven to be play – diversity, curiosity, and creativity.

From play comes experience. From experience develop insight and patterns of action – the link between the inner self and expression.

Perfectionism, comparison, and copying lead away from the freedom-nature of the human being. A shift in thinking is needed in the learning culture, so that technical skills, personality, and spiritual virtues can grow together, to bring forth the genius in every person.

The 3 Pillars of LernArt Pedagogy

Structure

Structural understanding of rhythm, harmony, and composition.

Transformation

Restructuring learning behavior. Developing an artistic attitude.

Research

Encountering the unknown and incorporating the superconscious levels into play.

The Art of Seeing Life as a Work of Art

Only through connection with our higher human – spiritual nature do we receive a Why for life, one that gives us the strength to serve the well-being of all and to transcend our personal life drama.

Only when placed in the service of humanity does art (music) mature into truly touching works that hold the power to guide humanity's course in the direction of its deepest longing.

Thus music becomes a place of inner contemplation. We search for our deepest inner motivations as human beings, to live and to create on this earth. Music becomes a reminder of our promise to ourselves: to bring our full creative potential to earth and to place full trust in the love that we ARE.

The Biggest Mistake Stems from Our Greatest Fear

"In all my years as a teacher, I observe: the militant desire for information – for patterns to be perfectly imitated."

Hardly anyone still knows how to truly learn. How to transform knowledge into experience. Nobody seems to notice that this is about art – the birthplace of creativity.

Individuality needs space. Yet most fear it. The Western mind was shaped in learning systems where imitation and conformity were considered virtues – and much of what sprang from our own will, our freedom-nature, was punished.

"Our deepest fear is not that we are inadequate.
Our deepest fear is that we are powerful beyond measure.
It is our light, not our darkness, that most frightens us.

We ask ourselves, who am I to be brilliant?
But actually, who are you not to be?
You are a child of God.

Your playing small does not serve the world.
There is nothing enlightened about shrinking so that other people won't feel insecure around you.

We are all meant to shine, as children do.
It's not just in some of us; it's in everyone.
And as we let our own light shine, we unconsciously give other people permission to do the same.

As we are liberated from our own fear, our presence automatically liberates others."

— Marianne Williamson, "A Return To Love"

Learning Goals

What You Develop at the School of Life

Not just learning to play – but becoming a musician.

It's not about memorizing patterns. It's about developing a musical personality that is authentic, free, and expressive.

Your Development Goals

Technical Mastery

Develop clean technique, precise timing, and the ability to fully command your instrument – as a tool for your expression, not as an end in itself.

Musical Understanding

Understand rhythm, harmony, and melody not just theoretically, but as a living language. Learn to intuitively grasp musical connections.

Creative Freedom

Overcome the fear of empty space. Develop the ability to improvise and play freely – creating in the moment.

Personal Expression

Find your musical voice. Develop a personal style that reflects your experiences, emotions, and your view of the world.

The Development Path

From the first touch to mastery – every stage has its own value.

Novice

The first encounter with the instrument. This is about discovery, curiosity, and getting to know the fundamental world of sound.

Beginner

Fundamentals of rhythm and technique. You learn to play confidently and steadily, developing your first own grooves.

Competent

Independent practice and composing. You understand musical connections and can work freely with the material.

Proficient

Deep practice and intuitive response. Music flows through you – technique and expression merge into one.

Expert

Mastery doesn't mean perfection, but complete presence. You pass on what you have received.

What You Concretely Take Away

Rhythmic Confidence

Steady groove, clean subdivisions, freedom in transitions and fill-ins.

Harmonic Understanding

Degrees of tension, resolutions, and the ability to consciously choose tonal colors.

Compositional Ability

Creating your own pieces that unite structure, expression, and meaning.

Presence in Playing

The ability to be fully in the moment – whether alone or with others.

The Four Keys to Mastery

Our programs are based on scientifically grounded principles used by the most successful coaches worldwide:

Modeling

Learning from Masters

"You can compress years into days by having the right mentor."

Instead of stumbling through years of trial and error, you learn directly from someone who has already walked the path. In our programs you learn not only from me, but also from the successes and challenges of your fellow students.

Total Immersion

Complete Immersion

"Not one hour per week for 50 weeks – but daily engagement over a defined period."

Total Immersion goes beyond superficial engagement. Your brain rewires itself when you fully immerse. Neuroplasticity accelerates when there are no other distractions.

Spaced Repetition

Repetition in Rhythm

"Constant, rhythmic repetition conditions mind and body for sustainable success."

The effectiveness is supported by cognitive psychology research: The brain benefits from repeated exposure with rest periods in between. Daily exercises (10–15 min) maximize long-term retention.

Community & Accountability

Community & Accountability

"Isolation is the killer of transformation – community is the catalyst."

You learn not only from the teacher, but also from each other. A coach holds you to a standard, measures your progress, and guides you in a way that makes sense for your needs. Even the best have coaches.

Scientifically proven:

Regular training increases productivity by 22%.
Training + personal coaching increases it by 88%.

"True transformation doesn't happen through information. It happens through immersion. Through diving in together. Through the accountability of a group. Through daily practice that becomes second nature."

The Two Pillars of Mentoring

Expression and Self-Confidence

Coming into full expression. Understanding which inner parts still hold you back – and showing what you truly are. Not playing louder, but more authentically.

"The quality of your expression is the experience you transmit into the world through your own work."

Consciousness Structures: New inner orders that transform your playing from within – not through rules, but through clarity.

Nils Böhm

Handpan Schule des Lebens was founded by Nils Böhm – musician, percussionist, and teacher with the deep conviction that music and inner growth are inseparably connected.

Philosophy

Nils' approach to music is deeply rooted in the conviction that creativity and spiritual practice are interwoven. He sees art not only as a means of expression, but as a path to connect with something greater than oneself.

"Experience the depth of your soul – through sound, through rhythm, through connection."

His work has a contemplative, introspective quality that invites the listener on a journey inward. It is not about reproducing patterns – but about finding your own voice and experiencing the handpan as a mirror of the soul.

The Path

As an experienced percussionist and handpan player, Nils has shared his knowledge in countless workshops, private lessons, and live sessions – both in Munich and online. Together with Peter Aumüller and other musicians, he forms the teaching team of Handpan Schule des Lebens.

Furthermore, Nils brings a unique perspective: Through 1.5 years of intensive work in the handpan ecosystem at the largest dealer in the DACH region, he has held many hundreds of instruments in his hands, studied building processes, and developed a deep understanding of makers, quality characteristics, and choosing the right instrument. This experience flows directly into his instrument selection advice.

One of his main concerns is helping others develop their own style and their own voice. He is convinced that every person has something valuable to offer – and that by tapping into one's own inner source of creativity, truly original and meaningful music can emerge.

The Vision: Handpan Schule des Lebens

At Handpan Schule des Lebens, Nils views learning the handpan as an alchemical process. It is about far more than technique: From thinking to feeling, you learn to use the analytical mind where it helps – and to let it go where it hinders the flow of music.

The handpan transforms from an object "out there" into an extension of your own body and emotional capacity for expression.

Nils guides his students not only in climbing the steps technically, but in experiencing them as a personal maturation process – step by step, toward an intuitive, free playing.

Why Quality Matters

The quality of a handpan influences far more than just the sound. It influences your entire playing behavior – and it is not only about technique, but above all about your inner state while playing.

After hundreds of hours of playing time and testing numerous handpans from a wide variety of makers, I can tell you: A truly well-built handpan draws you into its world. The sound touches something deep – almost as if you gain access to hidden spaces of your soul. That is exactly where your true playing begins.

High-quality instruments give energy back – through their beauty, their harmony, their coherent overtone structure. Cheap instruments, on the other hand, exhaust you over time: not physically, but mentally and emotionally. The sound gives you hardly anything back, no matter how deeply you listen.

Sound as Architecture of the Soul

Imagine the inner dome of an artfully designed cathedral. Everything is perfectly coordinated, every detail thought through. Just entering, you feel awe, wonder – and perhaps even a sense of the divine. That is how playing an excellently built handpan feels.

An inferior handpan, on the other hand, feels like a hastily assembled building without clear structure. The tonal lines don't meet, the harmonic arcs collapse upon themselves. You can sense: Love, experience, and care are missing here.

"Returning to the source of our naturalness – we can do this with an instrument that was built with love and mindfulness."

What do you want to connect with? Choosing the right handpan is a step that will shape your musical experience for years to come. Take your time – and get good advice.

How to Recognize True Quality

Almost every handpan sounds magical at the first note. But true quality does not reveal itself in the first moment – but in the connection you build with it over time.

Tuning Stability

How stable does the tuning remain over weeks and months? High-quality instruments stay in tune – mass-produced ones quickly lose clarity.

Tonal Balance

How balanced is the sound between the tone fields? Every note should respond equally and fit harmoniously into the overall picture.

Emotional Depth

How finely can the instrument be played emotionally? A good handpan responds to the subtlest nuances of your stroke and your intention.

What Makes a Good Maker

A high-quality handpan is like a work of art -- it is not produced at the push of a button but through a process of empathy, listening, retuning, and patience. An experienced maker allows the instrument to mature, listens to it again and again, and adjusts tiny nuances until the sound becomes round, clear, and alive.

Metallurgical knowledge, tuning experience, and a deep love of detail -- this combination makes the difference. Such knowledge lives on through direct exchange. The best handpans are created in small workshops with heart -- not in factories driven by profit.

Through 1.5 years of work at the largest handpan dealer in the German-speaking world, I know numerous makers personally, have visited their workshops, and gained deep insights into the building process. This knowledge flows directly into my consulting.
